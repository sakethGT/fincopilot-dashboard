> [!note] 🛂 NRI Tax Angle — Austin

> - **Default:** 30% flat withholding on gross rent — tenant must withhold and remit. No deductions allowed.
> - **Better option:** File Form W-8ECI and elect rental income as "Effectively Connected Income" (ECI). File Form 1040-NR annually. Now you can deduct mortgage interest, property taxes, depreciation (27.5-year schedule), insurance, maintenance → likely shows a **taxable loss** of $15K–$20K/year, which offsets other ECI income.
> - **DTAA Article 6** (India-US treaty): Immovable property income is taxed in the country where property is located (US). India does **not** tax this if you qualify as NRI under Indian law (outside India 182+ days/year).
> - **Depreciation recapture on sale:** IRS taxes the depreciation you claimed at 25% on sale — factor this into exit math.

## 🧠 Guru Framework Assessment

**Kiyosaki (Cash Flow lens):**
> US markets fail the Kiyosaki test completely at 6.38% rates — negative cash flow is the opposite of what he preaches. Hyderabad passes only weakly (positive CF but yield below India FD). **Only Garland, TX (>8% yield submarket) approaches Kiyosaki's definition of an investment.** The rest are speculative bets on appreciation. Kiyosaki would say: "Don't buy unless it pays you from day one."

**Dalio (Macro cycle lens):**
> We are in a late-cycle rate environment: US rates elevated (6.38% mortgage), RBI easing (5.25% repo, down 125bps). Dalio's framework says **falling rates = rising asset prices**. If/when the Fed cuts 100–150bps more, real estate values will re-rate upward and mortgage costs will drop — making today's purchase prices look cheap in 3–4 years. Hyderabad benefits more directly from RBI easing (cheaper home loans → more buyers).

**Graham (Margin of Safety lens):**
> **US:** GRM of 17.5–18.3 is at the lower end of fair value (15 = undervalued, 21 = overvalued). Cap rate of 1.45–1.87% vs Treasury 4.42% = you are paying a **significant premium for illiquidity** with no margin of safety on yield. Graham would not buy here.
> **India:** Gross yield at 3.88% vs India 10-yr G-Sec at ~7% = again paying premium over risk-free. But appreciation compensates if IT hiring holds. Graham would demand at least a 5%+ yield for margin of safety.

**NRI Lens (combined):**
> After applying NRI-specific costs — 15–30% TDS, stamp duty 7%, repatriation friction, currency depreciation risk — the true net return on India real estate is substantially lower than advertised yields. **The DTAA TRC filing is non-negotiable** — it saves 15% of every rupee of rent. US real estate avoids these costs but carries negative cash flow. In both cases, **direct real estate loses to VTI in pure 10-year return** unless you have significant capital for a debt-free purchase.
